Silenced by the System
By Tyrone King

When I began thinking about how to represent the overwhelming feeling of helplessness many people experience when dealing with insurance companies, I asked myself, “What does it feel like to be silenced?” That question led me to explore the emotional weight behind not just the system itself, but the lack of voice people feel within it. It's not just frustration — it’s a kind of quiet despair, where the conversation feels one-sided, unheard, and stuck.

Researching this topic was like opening Pandora’s box. The deeper I looked, the more layers I uncovered: red tape, denied claims, rising premiums, confusion, and a general lack of transparency. The complexity felt intentionally overwhelming — designed to disempower. As someone who isn’t a traditional visual artist, I turned to AI-generated images to help express these emotions. The image of a person with their mouth taped shut, surrounded by the names of powerful insurance companies, visually captures this feeling of being silenced and powerless in the face of bureaucracy. It’s a raw, symbolic representation of a system that many feel trapped within.
